Research

My research interest lies in semantics and its interfaces with morpho-syntax and pragmatics cross-linguistically. Currently, with Dr. Ivano Caponigro, I am working on Korean non-canonical interrogative clause constructions---echo questions (EQs), in particular. Korean EQs have unique morpho-syntactic, intonational, semantic, and pragmatic properties that have not been reported to be attested in other most-studied languages like English: namely, that EQs require a special clause-type marker that matches with the clause-type of the antecedent clause (e.g., declarative antecedent -- the marker tako, interrogative antecedent -- the marker nyako); that high rising final intonation is always accompanied with the EQ marker; that polar-EQs and wh-EQs use the same EQ marker; and so on. I aim to propose a novel semantic analysis that can properly capture those unique properties of Korean echo questions. Some preliminary works can be found below:

  • A semantic analysis for Korean echo questions
  • A Compositional Semantic Analysis of Echo Questions in Korean

Before coming to San Diego, I studied at Sungkyunkwan University in Seoul, Korea, and received M.A. in English Language and Literature. With Dr. Hanjung Lee, I investigated the effect of event structural meaning on English and Korean dative alternations through acceptability judgment experiments. Below are relevant works:

  • Verb Sensitivity of the English Dative Alternation: Experimental Evidence from Give-type Verbs
  • The realization of recipients of dative verbs in Korean: A stochastic Optimality-Theoretic Analysis

Miscellany

Have you ever seen this dog?

Now you have! This beautiful dog is 랑지 Rangji ([ɾaŋdʑi]), who was born in Korea in (probably) 2018, was rescued in 2020, and became my family in 2021. Her name came from the Korean word 꼬랑지 kkorangji, which means 'tail' in Kyungsang (Southeastern) dialect, for of course her awesome tail! Rangji is living with my family in Korea and I miss her so much.
